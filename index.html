Dimensions

Dimension is the physical type of a measurable quantity.

Three Fundamental Dimensions:

📏
[L]
Length
⚖️
[M]
Mass
⏱️
[T]
Time

Derived Dimensions:

Velocity: [L/T]   Force: [ML/T²]   Energy: [ML²/T²]

Units

Unit is the conventional quantity used to measure a dimension.

Length Units:

1 m = 100 cm = 3.281 ft = 39.37 in

Conversion Factor:

The ratio between two units of the same dimension with value = 1.

📏
1 m = 100 cm
📐
1 ft = 12 in
⚖️
1 kg = 1000 g

Unit Conversion & gc Factor

gc is the proportionality constant linking mass and force:

F = (m × a) / gc

System gc Value Units
SI 1 kg·m/(N·s²)
English 32.174 lbm·ft/(lbf·s²)

Dimensional Homogeneity

For an equation to be valid, all terms must have the same dimensions.

A + B = C

Required: [A] = [B] = [C]

Example:

F = m × a

[F] = [M][L/T²] = MLT⁻² ✓

Used to verify equations and derive units of unknown constants.
